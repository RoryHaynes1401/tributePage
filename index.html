Jack St. Clair Kilby

Jack Kilby was an electrical engineer who invented the integrated circuit and microchip, thus greatly influencing the shape of modern computing, which in turn greatly influenced the civilised world. Here's a time line of Jack Kilby's life:

  • Born on November 8, 1923, in Jefferson City, Missouri, Jack Kilby earned degrees in electrical engineering.
  • In 1958 at Texas Instruments, invented the integrated circuit, a historical, integral part of modern computing.
  • Having come up with the technology for pocket calculators as well, Kilby held many patents.
  • He was later awarded the 2000 Nobel Prize in Physics.
  • He died on June 20, 2005.
